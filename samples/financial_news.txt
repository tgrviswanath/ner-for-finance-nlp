Apple Inc. reported record quarterly revenue of $119.6 billion for Q1 2024, beating analyst expectations of $117.9 billion. CEO Tim Cook attributed the strong performance to robust iPhone 15 sales and continued growth in the Services segment, which reached $23.1 billion. The company's gross margin expanded to 45.9%, up from 42.8% in the same period last year. Apple also announced a $110 billion share buyback programme, the largest in the company's history.

The Federal Reserve held interest rates steady at 5.25%-5.50% at its March 2024 meeting, signalling a cautious approach to monetary policy amid persistent inflation. Fed Chair Jerome Powell indicated that the central bank needs greater confidence that inflation is moving sustainably toward its 2% target before cutting rates. The Fed's preferred inflation gauge, the PCE price index, rose 2.5% year-over-year in February. Markets are now pricing in fewer rate cuts in 2024 than previously anticipated.

Microsoft Corporation announced a $13 billion investment in OpenAI, deepening its partnership with the AI research company. The deal gives Microsoft exclusive rights to commercialise OpenAI's technologies, including GPT-4 and DALL-E. Microsoft's Azure cloud platform will serve as the primary infrastructure for OpenAI's products. The investment values OpenAI at approximately $86 billion, making it one of the most valuable private technology companies in the world.

Goldman Sachs reported Q4 2023 earnings per share of $5.48, significantly below analyst estimates of $8.44. The bank took a $1.3 billion write-down related to its consumer banking exit and recognised losses on its GreenSky lending platform. Revenue fell 12% year-over-year to $11.3 billion. CEO David Solomon acknowledged the challenges but expressed confidence in the firm's strategic refocus on its core investment banking and asset management businesses.

Nvidia's stock surged 16% after the company reported quarterly revenue of $22.1 billion, more than tripling year-over-year, driven by insatiable demand for its H100 AI chips. Data centre revenue reached $18.4 billion, up 409% from the prior year. The company guided for Q1 2024 revenue of approximately $24 billion, well above Wall Street expectations. Nvidia's market capitalisation briefly exceeded $2 trillion, making it one of the most valuable companies in the world.
